# TARAsense Sensory Attribute SOP v1

## Purpose
To standardize the selection and interpretation of sensory attributes for MSME product development using JAR methodology.

## Scope
Applicable to TARAsense Phase 1 (FAST-integrated MSME development).

## Rules
1. Overall acceptability (9-point hedonic) is mandatory.
2. A maximum of 5 attributes may be selected per test.
3. JAR attributes use a standardized 5-point JAR scale.
4. Attributes must be actionable and consumer-understandable.
5. One (1) custom attribute may be added per test, subject to validation.
6. Penalty analysis follows TARAsense Sensory Rules v1 thresholds.
7. Results are used for directional decision-making, not inferential claims.

## Standard 5-Point JAR Scale
1. Much too low
2. Slightly too low
3. Just about right
4. Slightly too high
5. Much too high

## Reporting Collapse Rule
- TOO LOW: 1-2
- JAR: 3
- TOO HIGH: 4-5

## Penalty Interpretation Thresholds
- Strong driver: Penalty >= 1.0 and non-JAR >= 20%
- Moderate driver: Penalty 0.5-0.99 and non-JAR >= 20%
- Not actionable: Penalty < 0.5 or non-JAR < 20%

## Workflow
1. MSME selects up to 5 candidate attributes.
2. MSME identifies top attributes to optimize (maximum 3 JAR attributes).
3. Remaining attributes are treated as observational for next iteration planning.
